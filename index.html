Education is Important

While many may argue against degrees nowadays, they are vital for teaching you to work hard, learn correctly, and try until you get the outcome you want.

The Importance of Layout

Before starting my journey into web design and development, I can honestly say I was one who jumped into creating the design for a website without even a thought of the end layout of the project.

Simplicity at its finest

While you may want to cram as much information in as possible, the less choices for the user to make, the overall better experience they will have. (Hick's Law)

The future is Responsive

Responsive design is THE most important thing when it comes to designing websites nowadays. A website that doesn't scale correctly for multiple platforms just won't cut it.

Perfection is Key

Close enough is simply not good enough. Do it right and perfectly the first time, or you will certainly be redoing it.

Design is an Iterative Process

You cannot simply go with the first hunch you had and call it good enough. More, More, More, MORE. You can NEVER have enough mockups.

Education and Career Article

Hello, my name is Connor Christensen and I am currently a full time student studying Web Design and Development at UVU. While doing this, I work at the office of IT Asset Management at UVU, as well as take on the role of Assistant Manager at a restaurant called Blaze Pizza. If there is anything that I pride myself on, it is my work ethic. As you many know, juggling all of these many responsibilites can be a challenge, but nothing is more important to me than getting a good education and hitting the ground running once offered a position in my field of interest.

Currently, I am hoping to get accepted into the upper division classes at UVU into the Web and App development program to continue to work on and hone my skills as a coder. My main goal is to be graduated come Fall of 2023 and, if it's not too crazy of a hope, to have a full time job in the field before finishing school. While attending UVU, my goals for grades have always been an A minimum, and I am happy to say that I have been almost perfect in that regard. While there are some slip ups here and there in the General Education courses, my Digital Media courses, specifically the coding heavy ones, have always been first on my priority to focus on and turn in truly the best work that I can. My goal is to graduate on the timeline that I have set, and graduate with high marks as that will truly show myself that I was able to complete the required courses excellently. This, I think, will also boost my confidence when entering into the job force.

If I had to set an entry-level career goal for myself, it would not be anything too unreasonable. As I mentioned, I am currently working at the IT Asset Management shop, which is great and allowing me the opportunity to gain more experience than I currently have, however it is more focued on the physical machines. My entry-level goal would be to work on the software side of IT. I have always loved learning new ways to solve problems, and being able to jump into a junior development job would be perfect for me to learn the ropes of the industy, gain confidence in the knowledge that I have, as well as give me more opportunity to grow and learn more. While there are no specific companies in mind, I am looking forward to a possible internship in the coming fall to begin my journey into the industry.

As a long-range career statement, I would like to be the head of a UX/UI team eventually. While this is a little more front end than what I currently emjoy doing, that is not to say that I dislike the design process whatsoever. I had never really found myself to be the creative type until starting this major. I found that it is a little easier to be creative on a computer when you can have your designs be exactly the way you had invisioned them. While that will take a lot of experience and practice to work my way up to, starting on the back end of development would be all I need to be happy. That being said, before making the leap to a team lead on a UX project, I would like to be the Senior Developer on a project. This gives me the opportunity to really let myself shine, as well as learn to teach and mentor those who are new to the development game.
